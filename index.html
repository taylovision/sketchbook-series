About the Series

Sketchbook Series is a 2-D workshop with a focus on foundational studies in drawing, collage, and painting. The series is aimed at beginners who are looking to better understand different processes or want to supplement what they already know with additional techniques.

What Will I learn?

The series is split into three disciplines with two classes per discipline. Participants are encouraged to take the full series, but have the flexibility to pick and choose which classes they would like to take.

Recommended art materials list.

Details about each class are outlined below.

This class is geared to be a no-pressure introduction into drawing with a focus on contour line, value, and ‘Drawing on the Right Side of the Brain’ exercises.

  • First Hour: Timed 1, 5, and 10 minute drawings switching from contour line techniques to value/shading techniques.
  • Second Hour: Two 30 minute hand drawings using a transparency grid (Betty Edwards, Drawing on the Right Side of the Brain).
  • Take away - 2 hand drawings

The second drawing class builds upon the first but is still approachable for the beginning artist.

  • First Hour: Drawing warm up: timed contour line drawings and shading/value exercises.
  • Second Hour: We set up a still life and complete timed drawings that are “zoomed in” compositions (different parts of the still-life) creating mini drawing compositions. Students will use a grid (Betty Edwards, Drawing on the Right Side of the Brain) to assist them.
  • Take away - still-life zoom drawings (4-5 drawings)

The first collage class is an introduction to mixed media artwork with a focus on paper transfers and fabric similar to Pop Art and DaDa.

  • First Hour: Exploration of different techniques: photo transfers using Modge Podge, Matte Medium, and magazine collage.
  • Second Hour: We will complete an Exquisite Corpse activity, and create tiny collages using that game as inspiration.
  • Take away - small collage

The second collage class will focus on the collage narrative or zine. It is necessary for each attendee to have a topic in mind - a music focused zine, poetry, art, or comics?

  • First Hour: Writing workshop, fleshing out ideas, and cultivating stream of consciousness to create text for zine creation.
  • Second Hour: Zine-making.
  • Take away - small collage, or zine

Basics of wet on wet, wet on dry, stamping, using frisket, and salt/alcohol effects primarily using watercolors.

  • First Hour: Watercolor basics.
    • Second hour the attendees will be guided through two small watercolor paintings - one of fruit and one of flowers.
  • Take away - small watercolor paintings.

The last class will focus on adding pen and ink detail to a watercolor painting.

  • First Hour: Consists of painting several large abstract compositions.
  • Second Hour: Attendees will cut their composition into smaller pieces and add pen and ink contour drawing atop their abstract creations.
  • Take away - small ink drawing
